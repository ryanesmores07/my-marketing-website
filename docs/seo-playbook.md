# SEO Playbook

This portfolio already includes the code-side SEO basics:

- Localized page metadata for `/en` and `/jp`
- Canonical and hreflang alternates
- Structured data for `WebSite`, `Person`, and `ProfessionalService`
- Clean `robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml`
- Clearer internal section structure and anchor targets
- Natural English and Japanese copy on the core landing page

## Off-Page Actions To Do Manually

These items cannot be completed from code alone and should only be done with real accounts, real relationships, and real business information.

1. Verify the domain in Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools, then submit the sitemap.
2. Update LinkedIn, GitHub, Upwork, and any active freelance profiles so they link to the portfolio and describe the same service positioning.
3. Ask clients for attributed backlinks only where public credit is appropriate, such as partner pages, founder bios, or public case-study mentions.
4. Publish supporting content that can earn links naturally, such as Shopify migration notes, bilingual ecommerce SEO insights, or paid ads learnings from real work.
5. Keep the brand name, service wording, and location references consistent across your website and external profiles.
6. If you want local SEO in Japan, decide on the exact business name, service area, and public business profile details before creating local citations or a Google Business Profile.

## What To Avoid

- Do not add fake reviews, fake citations, or placeholder verification tokens.
- Do not use client names publicly unless you have permission to do so.
- Do not create directory listings with inconsistent business details.
- Do not promise rankings, ROAS, or response times you cannot support.
